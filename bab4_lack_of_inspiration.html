Bab 4: Lack of Inspiration

Tingkat: [Mahir]

BAGIAN 1: KOSA KATA PENTING

  • Dwell (verb): Terpaku pada pikiran; memikirkan sesuatu terus-menerus.
  • Hindered (verb, past tense): Terhalang; terhambat.
  • Vice versa (adverb): Dan sebaliknya.
  • Accomplish (verb): Menyelesaikan; mencapai sesuatu.
  • Aggravate (verb): Memperburuk; membuat jengkel.
  • Arise (verb): Muncul; timbul.
  • Rut (noun): Kebiasaan atau pola yang membosankan dan sulit diubah.
  • Sprouts (noun): Tunas; kecambah.
  • Bogus (adjective): Palsu; gadungan.
  • Soil (noun): Tanah.

BAGIAN 2: CERITA PENDEK

A Morning at the Market

Rian felt like he was stuck in a deep creative rut. For weeks, his paintbrush had remained untouched, and his canvas was empty. He knew he shouldn't dwell on his lack of inspiration, as that would only aggravate the situation. So, when his mother asked him to go to the market, he reluctantly agreed. Perhaps a change of scenery would help.

His mission was simple: accomplish the grocery list before noon. However, his progress was immediately hindered by the bustling morning crowd. The narrow aisles were packed with shoppers and vendors, and the air was thick with the scent of spices and fresh produce.

A new problem was about to arise. A slick salesman tried to sell him a bottle of "premium" olive oil at a ridiculously high price. Rian examined the label; it looked cheap and poorly printed. He was certain the product was bogus and quickly walked away.

Feeling a bit defeated, he stopped at a vegetable stall. His eyes wandered over the colourful array of goods and landed on a small tray of bean sprouts. He watched the tiny green shoots pushing their way out of the dark, damp soil. They were so full of life and determination, a stark contrast to his own stagnant energy.

A thought struck him. He had been seeing his creative block as an obstacle, but maybe it was just a resting period, like soil before new seeds grow. He realized he needed the market's chaotic energy to feel alive, and the market needed customers like him to thrive. It was a symbiotic relationship, and vice versa.

With a newfound perspective, Rian quickly finished his shopping. He had not only accomplished his task but had also found the spark he was looking for. He couldn't wait to go home and let his new ideas sprout onto the canvas.
